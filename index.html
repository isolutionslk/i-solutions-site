Executive Dashboard

The command centre of the entire POS system. The Executive Dashboard gives management a live, real-time overview of every key performance indicator — revenue, profit, inventory value, and refund exposure — all on a single screen without needing to run a single report.

Used by: Management & Owners
Updates in real-time
No manual refresh needed
Full financial snapshot
Refund leakage tracking

Lifetime Net Revenue

Total sales amount minus all refunded transactions. Always accurate, always live — the true picture of your business's earnings since day one.

Profit Efficiency %

Visual progress bar showing the ratio of profit to revenue. Instantly tells management whether the business is operating at healthy margins.

Inventory Capital Value

Total buying cost of all current stock vs. potential sell-through value. Know exactly how much capital is tied up on the shelf at any moment.

Unrealized Profit

Estimated margin on all stock currently in inventory. Calculated using the margin ratio from product costs — giving you the profit sitting on the shelves.

Today's Earnings

Live display of today's total sales. Updates instantly with every completed transaction so management always knows the day's performance.

Revenue Leakage Tracker

Total cumulative value lost to customer refunds. Tracks financial exposure from reversals so management can see patterns and address root causes.

Technical Logic

Virtual Profit Calculation
When recorded profit is zero but revenue exists, the system calculates a margin ratio from inventory (sell value minus cost ÷ sell value) and applies it as an estimated profit figure.
Real-Time Updates
All KPIs query the live database on every page load and refresh automatically — no caching, no stale data. Every completed sale reflects immediately.
Leakage Calculation
Sums the net value of all transactions marked as Refunded or Voided. Updated the moment a refund is processed.
Avg. Transaction Value
Total completed sales revenue divided by number of completed transactions. Excludes voided and returned transactions from the count.

Point of Sale Billing

Used by: Cashiers
Barcode & name search

Step-by-Step Sale Process

1
Open the Billing Screen

Cashier navigates to Point of Sale. A clean, empty cart is immediately ready to accept items — no loading screens or setup required.

2
Add Products to Cart

Scan a barcode or type the product name. The system instantly retrieves the price, applicable tax rate, and current stock level. Out-of-stock items are flagged immediately.

3
Apply Discounts

Apply a discount to a single line item (percentage or fixed amount) or across the entire basket. Any active promotions configured by management are automatically shown and selectable.

4
Select Payment Method

Choose Cash, Card, or Split Payment. For cash, the system calculates change due. For split payments, it handles both portions independently and calculates the correct change for the cash portion.

5
Complete & Receipt Prints

The sale is recorded with a unique invoice number. A full itemised receipt prints automatically — no additional steps required. Stock levels drop and the dashboard updates in real time.

Automatic Receipt Printing

Receipt includes store details, full item list with quantities and prices, discount applied, payment method, change due, and a brand QR code. Zero extra steps for the cashier.

Receipt Reprint

Any past receipt can be reprinted at any time using the invoice number — essential when customers request duplicate receipts for expenses or returns.

Split Payment Logic

When a sale is split between cash and card, the system handles both portions. If the cash amount entered is zero, it defaults to full net payable — preventing negative change calculations.

Live Stock Check

Every product lookup includes a live stock availability check. Cashiers are instantly alerted if an item is low or out of stock before adding it to the cart.

Sales, Reports & Financial Analysis

A comprehensive suite of reporting tools covering every financial dimension of your retail operation. From raw transaction history to detailed profit-per-item analysis, tax collection summaries, and Excel export — all in one Reports Hub accessible to authorised staff.

Used by: Management, Accountants

Sales History

Complete chronological list of all transactions with date, customer name, invoice number, payment status, and final amount. Searchable by date range, customer, or invoice number.

Sales Detail Report

Line-by-line breakdown of every item sold: buying cost (WAC frozen at sale), selling price, gross profit, and margin percentage. The most powerful profitability tool in the system.

One-Click Excel Export

Exports the full Sales Detail Report as a tab-separated file with columns pre-formatted for accounting use. Returned and refunded items are automatically identified and their figures negated.

Tax Report

Tax collected grouped by rate (e.g. 15% VAT) with a date-range filter. Designed specifically for periodic tax filing — shows exactly how much tax was collected per rate band.

Item Sales History

Per-product historical analysis — how many units sold, at what price, with what cost (frozen at time of each sale) and resulting profit. Essential for identifying top and bottom performers.

Invoice Details

Full breakdown of any single invoice: all line items, quantities, unit prices, discount applied, tax, and net payable amount — with an option to reprint the original receipt.

Technical Logic

Frozen WAC Cost
Weighted Average Cost is captured and permanently saved at the moment of each sale line item. Historical profit figures remain accurate even when product costs change later.
Dynamic Filtering
SQL conditions are built dynamically from active filters. Search terms use partial matching; status filters use exact matching. All parameters safely bound to prevent SQL injection.
Tax Aggregation
Tax collected is calculated by multiplying each line item total by its product's configured tax rate, then grouped and summed by rate band — giving precise figures per tax category.
Excel Export Logic
Sets download headers then outputs tab-separated rows. Returned items identified by status have their revenue and profit figures negated to correctly reflect reversals in accounting exports.

Inventory & Stock Control

Real-time visibility of every product across all storage locations. Colour-coded alerts make stock management effortless — managers see at a glance which products need reordering and which locations are running low, without manually checking shelves.

Used by: Store Managers

Multi-Location Tracking

Stock quantities tracked separately per storage location. View a product's availability across all locations simultaneously — essential for businesses with multiple storage areas or branches.

Colour-Coded Alerts

Green = healthy stock level. Amber = approaching the low-stock threshold. Red = zero stock, out of stock. Instantly identifies which products require urgent attention without reading numbers.

WAC Cost Per Product

Buying cost and selling price maintained individually per product using Weighted Average Cost. Used in profit calculations across all reports and frozen at the time of each sale.

Full Movement Audit

Every stock change — whether from a completed sale, a received purchase order, or a stock removal — is logged automatically, creating a permanent, traceable history of every unit.

Tax Rate Per Product

Each product has its own configured tax rate. Used to calculate tax on every sale line item and rolled up into the Tax Report — ensuring tax compliance per product category.

Return Stock Module

Remove stock for supplier returns, damage, expiry, or inventory corrections. Every removal is wrapped in a database transaction and logged as a negative quantity entry for full traceability.

Purchase Order Management

Manages the complete lifecycle of supplier orders — from creation through receipt. When goods are marked as received, inventory updates automatically across all modules. Full supplier order history is available at any time.

Used by: Purchasing, Managers

Purchase Order Lifecycle

1
Create the Order

Raise a new PO directly in the system. Add the supplier name, all line items with quantities and unit costs. The system assigns a unique PO number automatically.

2
Status: Pending → Ordered

Once submitted to the supplier, update the order status to Ordered. Edit quantities or line items at any point before goods arrive.

3
Mark Goods Received

When delivery arrives, mark the order as Received. Inventory stock levels update automatically — no manual stock entry required. The WAC cost recalculates across affected products.

4
Full Supplier History

Every order per supplier is stored and accessible. View order dates, quantities, costs, and status for any supplier at any time — essential for supplier negotiations and audit trails.

Technical Logic

Safe Order Deletion
Uses a database transaction to delete all order line items first, then the order header. If either step fails, both are rolled back — preventing orphaned records in the database.
Auto Inventory Update
On marking received, each line item quantity is added to the corresponding product's stock count. The transaction is atomic — all items update or none do.

Gift Card Management

Issue, track, and redeem unique gift cards. Every card gets a guaranteed unique 16-character code, is active immediately after creation, and maintains a full redemption history. An ideal tool for customer loyalty and promotions.

Used by: Marketing, Managers, Cashiers

Unique Code Generation

Creates a random 16-character alphanumeric code, verifies uniqueness against the entire database before saving, then formats it as four groups of four: XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX.

Balance & Expiry Control

Set the initial balance per card and optionally set an expiry date. Cards can be created with any value — flexible for seasonal promotions, loyalty rewards, or refund credits.

Full Redemption Tracking

Every use of a gift card is recorded against the card, with date, amount redeemed, and remaining balance. Full transaction history viewable per card.

Gift Card Report

A comprehensive report showing all issued cards, current balances, expiry dates, and full redemption history — giving management complete visibility over the gift card programme.

Promotions Management

Create and manage time-limited discount campaigns. Link promotions to specific products or apply them across the full catalogue. Only active, in-date promotions appear at the billing screen — expired ones are hidden automatically, eliminating cashier error.

Used by: Marketing, Managers

Date-Controlled Campaigns

Every promotion has a defined start and end date. Once the end date passes, the promotion is automatically hidden from the billing screen — no manual intervention required.

Product-Linked Discounts

Link a promotion to specific products only, or apply it across the entire product catalogue. Full flexibility to design targeted or store-wide discount campaigns.

Instant Deactivation

Deactivate any promotion with a single click — immediately removes it from the billing screen. Historical sales data linked to that promotion is never affected.

Edit Without Impact

Edit promotion details, dates, or discount amounts at any time. Changes apply from that point forward only — past sales data and applied discounts remain unchanged.

Return Stock

A dedicated module for removing stock from inventory with full accountability. Whether it's a supplier return, damaged goods, an expired product, or an inventory correction — every removal is logged, reversible, and permanently auditable.

Used by: Store Managers

Supplier Returns

Remove stock being returned to a supplier. Records the product, quantity, reason, and date — giving a clear paper trail for supplier credit claims and reconciliations.

Damaged & Expired Goods

Remove damaged or expired stock from the live inventory count. Keeps stock figures accurate and ensures out-of-date products cannot be sold at the POS.

Database Transaction Safety

Every stock removal is wrapped in a database transaction. On success, a negative quantity entry is written to the transaction log. If anything fails, the removal is fully rolled back.

Permanent Audit Record

Creates a permanent, reversible audit record. Management can view the complete history of all stock removals with dates, quantities, products, and reasons at any time.

User Login & Security

Role-based access control built into the foundation of every module. Each staff member has their own login credentials. The system loads their specific permissions on login — they only ever see and access the modules their role permits.

Used by: All Staff

Individual Credentials

Every staff member has a unique username and password. Shared accounts are not permitted — ensuring every action in the system is traceable to a specific person.

Permission-Based Access

On login, the system reads a comma-separated permissions list from the user record and loads it into the session. Every protected page checks this list before granting access.

Module Visibility Control

The navigation menu dynamically shows only the modules each user's account permits. Unauthorised modules are completely hidden — users cannot navigate to pages they don't have access to.

Full Action Audit Log

Every significant action — sales, refunds, stock changes, PO receipts, gift card redemptions — is logged against the logged-in user, creating a permanent, searchable audit trail.

Technical Logic

Session Permission Array
Permissions stored as a comma-separated string in the user record are loaded into a PHP session array at login. Every page performs an array_search check before rendering content.
Page-Level Protection
Each module page independently verifies session permissions. Direct URL access to protected pages is blocked — security is enforced at the server, not just the UI.

Processing a Sale

Open the billing screen, scan or search each product, verify the price and stock, apply any discounts, select the payment method (Cash, Card, or Split), and complete the transaction. The receipt prints automatically.

Tip: If an item shows zero stock, do not complete the sale — inform the store manager immediately.

Applying Discounts

You can apply a discount to a single item (percentage or fixed amount) or to the entire basket. Active promotions created by management appear automatically — select them directly without typing any discount codes.

Tip: Only management can create or modify promotions — if a customer requests a discount not in the system, escalate to your manager.

Handling Split Payments

Select Split Payment to accept part cash, part card. The system calculates the correct change for the cash portion automatically. Never manually calculate split payment change — always let the system handle it.

Tip: The system prevents negative change from displaying — if it shows zero change, the card covers the remainder.

Reprinting Receipts

If a customer needs a duplicate receipt, navigate to Receipt Reprint and enter the original invoice number. The full original receipt prints immediately — no manager approval needed.

Tip: Every receipt shows the invoice number at the top — always ask the customer to keep it for any future reprints.

Monitoring the Dashboard

Check the Executive Dashboard at the start and end of every shift. Review today's earnings, profit efficiency percentage, refund leakage, and any low-stock alerts. This single screen tells you the health of your entire operation.

Tip: High refund leakage is a signal to investigate — check Sales History to identify patterns in refunded transactions.

Managing Inventory

Review the Inventory module daily. Red items need immediate attention — raise a Purchase Order before stock runs out. Amber items need to be monitored. Use the colour alerts to prioritise your reordering decisions.

Tip: Set low-stock thresholds appropriately for each product's sales velocity — fast movers need higher thresholds.

Processing Stock Returns

When removing stock for supplier returns, damage, or expiry — always use the Return Stock module. Never adjust stock manually. Every removal creates a permanent audit record that protects both you and the business.

Tip: Include a clear reason note for every return — this is your audit trail for supplier credit claims.

Running Promotions

Create promotions with defined start and end dates before campaigns go live. Link them to specific products or the full catalogue. Once created, cashiers see them automatically — no briefing needed. Deactivate instantly when a promotion ends early.

Creating a Purchase Order

Navigate to Purchase Orders and create a new PO. Enter the supplier name, then add each line item with product, quantity, and unit cost. The system assigns a unique PO number. Save the order — its status is set to Pending automatically.

Tip: You can edit quantities at any time before marking the order as received — always verify quantities match the delivery note.

Receiving a Delivery

When goods arrive, locate the PO in the system. Verify quantities match what was delivered. Mark the order as Received — inventory stock levels update automatically for every line item. No manual stock entry is needed.

Tip: Only mark as Received once goods are physically confirmed — this is the moment stock enters your system count.

Supplier History

All orders per supplier are permanently stored and accessible. Use supplier history to verify past order quantities, costs, and delivery patterns — essential data for supplier negotiations and price comparisons.

Generating the Sales Detail Report

Navigate to Sales Detail Report, set your date range, and click Export. The system produces a tab-separated file with buying cost, selling price, profit, and margin per line item. Returned items are already negated in the export — ready for your accounting software.

Tip: Run this report monthly and reconcile it against your bank statements. The WAC costs are frozen — figures will not change even if costs update later.

Preparing Tax Filing

Go to the Tax Report, filter by your VAT filing period, and review tax collected by rate. The system groups and sums tax by rate band — giving you precisely the figures required for VAT returns without any manual calculation.

Tip: Cross-reference with Sales History to verify total sales figures match your tax collected — any discrepancy needs investigation.

Invoice Verification & Audit

Use Invoice Details to pull up any individual transaction. See all line items, quantities, unit prices, discount applied, tax, and net payable. Use Sales History to search by date or invoice number for reconciliation and audit requests.

Issuing Gift Cards

Navigate to Gift Cards and create a new card. The system generates a unique 16-character code automatically — formatted as XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX. Set the balance and optional expiry date. The card is active at the POS immediately after saving.

Tip: Always print or note the card code for the customer — they cannot redeem without it. The code is only shown once at creation.

Creating a Promotion

Go to Promotions and create a new campaign. Give it a name, set the start and end dates, choose the discount type (percentage or fixed), and link it to specific products or the full catalogue. Once the start date is reached, cashiers see it automatically at the billing screen.

Tip: Set end dates carefully — the system hides expired promotions automatically. Test the promotion in the billing screen after creation to verify it appears correctly.

Tracking Gift Card Performance

The Gift Card Report shows all issued cards with current balances, expiry dates, and complete redemption history. Use this to measure campaign effectiveness, identify unused balances, and report on the overall gift card programme performance.
